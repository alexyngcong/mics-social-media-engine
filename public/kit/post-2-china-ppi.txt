That is China's producer-price inflation print for April, the fastest pace in 45 months and the second consecutive monthly gain, against a market consensus of 1.5%. The National Bureau of Statistics tied the move to mining (+10.8%), raw materials (+7.1%), and processing-stage cost pressure, all transmitting directly from the Iran-war energy shock through to Chinese factory gates.

For UAE importers from China, the supplier-repricing wave should land within 60 to 90 days. The firms with rolling short-cycle PO frameworks will absorb the move faster than those with annual price commitments. The deeper issue is that this is a supply-side shock, not a demand recovery, so margin defence has to come from contract structure rather than from passing it forward.

Procurement teams that have not cycled through their top 20 suppliers with revised cost models are running on stale assumptions. Tightening PO frequency, locking spot where contracts allow, and re-modelling landed cost on a USD basis will help. Hoping it passes will not.

Scenario planning that uses last cycle's assumptions underprices the regional transmission. The Gulf sits at the crossover of monetary, energy, and trade policy in ways that compound rather than offset.
