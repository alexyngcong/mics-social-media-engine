The bond desks at Alphabet and Amazon both moved to new currencies today, and the read for UAE treasury teams is bigger than the headlines suggest.

Alphabet is preparing its first yen-denominated bond through Mizuho, Bank of America, and Morgan Stanley. Amazon is preparing a six-part Swiss franc offering across three- to twenty-five-year maturities, with BNP Paribas, Deutsche Bank, and JPMorgan as mandated banks. Both deals are explicitly framed as AI-infrastructure capex financing, and they sit alongside earlier euro and Canadian-dollar prints that have pushed Big Tech's debt-funded AI capex toward USD 700 billion in 2026.

The signal underneath: multi-currency funding is no longer a fringe treasury tactic for premium issuers. Long-duration capex stories are being absorbed by the JPY and CHF investor base at acceptable spreads. For UAE issuers sizing programmes against ADQ, Mubadala, or MoIAT industrial-financing pipelines, the prints are a usable benchmark for both tenor structure and the currency-mix conversation that is becoming a board-level item.

Funding-mix reviews need a quarterly cadence in this environment, not an annual one. Issuance windows, deposit beta, and refinancing pipelines are moving faster than legacy treasury policy frameworks anticipate.
